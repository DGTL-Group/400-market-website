# 400 Market — Brand Voice & Visual Identity Guidelines

> Source: Official Brand Book v11 (November 2024) + SVG logo files
> Compiled by DGTL Group · April 2026

---

## Brand Overview

400 Market is an established weekend market in Ontario, Canada (Est. 1986). It is a community gathering place for food, artisan finds, and local vendors. The brand identity is bold, energetic, and approachable — rooted in authenticity and community.

**Tagline:** FOOD, FINDS & FUN. OPEN EVERY WEEK-END.

---

## Logo

### Primary Mark
Shield/badge shape with:
- "400" in large bold condensed numerals (top)
- "MARKET" in white on black horizontal banner (middle)
- "EST 1986" on an orange curved tab at the bottom

### Logo Variants
| Variant | Usage |
|---|---|
| **Full Colour** | All primary brand applications; use on white, light, or yellow backgrounds |
| **Dark / Positive** | All `#2C2C2C` — use on white or light backgrounds (<50% grey) |
| **Light / Negative** | All `#FFFFFF` — use on dark backgrounds (>50% grey) or dark photography |
| **On Photography** | White logo on dark/moody photos; dark logo on light/bright photos |

### Logo Safe Area
Maintain a minimum clear space equal to one full shield-width on all sides of the logo. Never crowd the mark with other graphic elements.

### Logo Don'ts
- Do not apply the full-colour logo on patterned, textured, or photographic backgrounds
- Do not distort, rotate, or recolour the logo
- Do not use the logo at sizes too small to read the "EST 1986" text

### Secondary / Accent Colours
| Name | Hex | RGB | Usage |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Orange** | `#F7941D` | 247, 148, 29 | Accents, CTA buttons, highlight elements |
| **Mango Tango** | `#E57200` | 229, 114, 0 | Deep accent, hover states, bold callouts |
| **Granite Gray** | `#606060` | 96, 96, 96 | Supporting text, dividers, subtle UI elements |

### Colour Principles
- Yellow is the **predominant colour** — the market's primary identity colour
- Orange and Mango Tango are **complements**, not dominants
- Avoid using orange as a primary background at large scale
- High-contrast combinations (yellow/black, white/dark gray) are preferred for readability

## Typography

### Primary — METAFORA
- **Designer:** Dirtyline Studio
- **Role:** Headlines, display type, section labels, branding, logo lockups, posters, packaging
- **Character:** Elegant yet playful; works in all-caps for display use
- **Weights used:** Regular, SemiBold, Black
- **Style:** Condensed sans-serif display; unique letterforms with personality

### Secondary — DM Sans
- **Role:** Body copy, descriptions, captions, UI text, form labels, supporting content
- **Character:** Multifunctional, highly legible; works equally well in print and on screen
- **Weights used:** All weights available (Regular, Medium, SemiBold, Bold)
- **Style:** Geometric humanist sans-serif; clean, neutral, approachable

### Typography Rules
- **Never** use DM Sans for primary headlines — that is METAFORA's role
- METAFORA should generally be set in **ALL CAPS** for headlines
- Body text should be set in DM Sans at comfortable reading sizes (16px+ on web)
- Maintain clear typographic hierarchy: METAFORA headline → DM Sans subhead/body

## Iconography

### Icon Style
- Flat, minimalist line icons
- Clean geometric lines; no gradients or shadows
- Single stroke weight; versatile across all sizes
- Black (`#2C2C2C`) on light backgrounds; White on dark backgrounds
- Icon set covers market categories: food, produce, crafts, apparel, home goods, services

### Icon Usage
- Icons are supplemental — never replace text labels in primary navigation
- Use consistently at the same size within a given layout
- Maintain adequate padding/breathing room around icons

## Brand Voice & Tone

*Note: The official brand book is a visual identity manual. The voice guidelines below are derived from the tagline, tone of applications, and brand personality evident in the visual identity.*

### Brand Personality
| We Are | We Are Not |
|---|---|
| Bold and direct | Timid or corporate |
| Warm and community-first | Cold or transactional |
| Energetic and fun | Stuffy or overly formal |
| Authentic and established | Trendy or chasing novelty |
| Clear and unpretentious | Jargon-heavy or complicated |
| Proud of local roots | Generic or placeless |

### Voice Constants (apply always)
- **Approachable:** Speak like a friendly vendor, not a corporation
- **Energetic:** Short punchy sentences; active voice; forward momentum
- **Welcoming:** Every visitor — shopper, vendor, first-timer — should feel invited
- **Direct:** State the value clearly; don't bury the lead
- **Community-proud:** Reference the market experience, local vendors, and the tradition

### Tone Flex (adjust by context)
| Context | Tone |
|---|---|
| Hero / Homepage | Bold, inviting, high energy — short punchy lines |
| Vendor Directory | Warm, descriptive, story-forward |
| Events | Exciting, specific, action-oriented |
| FAQ / Practical Info | Clear, plain, helpful — no fluff |
| Become a Vendor | Persuasive, community-focused, encouraging |
| Legal / Privacy | Neutral, clear, CASL-compliant |
| Error / 404 | Light, friendly, never frustrating |

### Language Do's
- Use "we" and "our market" — this is a community space
- Lead with the experience: sights, smells, energy of the market
- Name specific things: vendors, categories, weekends, seasons
- Use sentence fragments for punch: "Fresh produce. Local crafts. Every weekend."
- Embrace the ampersand (&) — it's in the tagline for a reason

### Language Don'ts
- Don't use corporate language: "leverage", "synergy", "solutions"
- Don't be vague: "great selection" → be specific about what's there
- Don't over-promise: keep claims grounded in real experience
- Don't use passive voice in headlines or CTAs
- Don't call vendors "vendors" to their faces in marketing copy — use "makers", "growers", "artisans", or their business names

### Signature Phrases / Reference Copy
- **Tagline:** FOOD, FINDS & FUN. OPEN EVERY WEEK-END.
- **Market email:** manager@400market.com
- **Website:** www.400market.com

## Applications Summary

### What the brand looks like in use
- **Business cards:** Yellow background / black background alternating; METAFORA name, DM Sans contact details
- **Brochures:** Yellow + white + dark gray sections; full-colour logo; photography of market scenes
- **Posters:** Bold METAFORA headlines; yellow/orange/dark backgrounds; logo prominent
- **Web:** Yellow hero sections, clean white content areas, dark gray footers

### Photography Direction
- Candid, lively market scenes — people browsing, vendors selling, products displayed
- Warm, natural light; authentic moments over staged shots
- Subject matter: market floor, vendor booths, fresh produce, handmade goods, community atmosphere
- Avoid: stock-photo feel, overly polished/corporate shots, empty market scenes
